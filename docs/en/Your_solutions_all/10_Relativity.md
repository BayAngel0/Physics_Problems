## 1. Length Contraction

Length contraction formula:

$$
L = L_0 \sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}
$$

Given:

$$
L_0 = 1 \text{ m}, \quad L = 0.5 \text{ m}
$$

$$
0.5 = 1 \sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}
$$

$$
0.25 = 1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}
$$

$$
\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 0.75
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{0.75}c
$$

$$
v \approx 0.866c
$$

# 2. Time Dilation

Time dilation formula:

$$
t = \gamma t_0
$$

Lorentz factor:

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
$$

Given:

$$
t = 2t_0
$$

Thus:

$$
\gamma = 2
$$

$$
2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
$$

$$
1-\frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{1}{4}
$$

$$
\frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{3}{4}
$$

$$
v = 0.866c
$$

# 7. Velocity Transformation

Rocket speed:

$$
v = 0.8c
$$

Probe speed relative to rocket:

$$
u = 0.5c
$$

### Classical mechanics

$$
v_{classical} = 0.8c + 0.5c
$$

$$
v = 1.3c
$$

(Not physically possible)

---

### Special relativity

Velocity addition formula:

$$
v = \frac{u+v}{1+\frac{uv}{c^2}}
$$

$$
v = \frac{0.5c+0.8c}{1+0.5\times0.8}
$$

$$
v = \frac{1.3c}{1.4}
$$

$$
v \approx 0.93c
$$

# 8. Adding Velocities

If one object moves at the speed of light:

$$
v = c
$$

Using relativistic velocity addition:

$$
u' = \frac{u+c}{1+\frac{uc}{c^2}}
$$

Result:

$$
u' = c
$$

Thus the speed of light remains constant and **cannot be exceeded**.

# 9. Twin Paradox (ISS)

ISS speed:

$$
v = 7.66 \text{ km/s}
$$

Convert to fraction of light speed:

$$
\frac{v}{c} \approx 2.55\times10^{-5}
$$

Lorentz factor approximation:

$$
\gamma \approx 1 + \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{v}{c}\right)^2
$$

Difference in time after one year:

$$
\Delta t \approx 0.01 \text{ s}
$$

Astronaut is about **10 milliseconds younger**.
